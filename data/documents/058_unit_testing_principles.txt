Unit tests verify the behavior of a single function, method, or class in isolation from
its dependencies. Good unit tests are fast, isolated, repeatable, self-validating, and
timely, following the FIRST principles. Dependencies are typically replaced with test
doubles such as mocks, stubs, or fakes to isolate the unit under test. Each test should
cover a single behavior and follow the Arrange-Act-Assert structure, making it easy to
understand what is being tested and why a failure matters.

Semantic Versioning (SemVer) is a versioning convention that assigns version numbers in
the format MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH to software releases. A MAJOR version increment signals a
breaking change incompatible with the previous API, a MINOR increment adds functionality
in a backward-compatible manner, and a PATCH increment indicates backward-compatible bug
fixes. Pre-release identifiers and build metadata can be appended after a hyphen or plus
sign respectively. SemVer provides a shared contract between package authors and
consumers, making it possible to manage dependency upgrades predictably.

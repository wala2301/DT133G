The Observer design pattern defines a one-to-many dependency so that when one object (the
subject) changes state, all dependents (observers) are notified automatically. This is
the structural basis for event-driven systems, pub/sub messaging, and reactive programming
frameworks. In Python, observer behavior is commonly implemented with callback lists or
event emitters in libraries such as pyee. The pattern promotes loose coupling because the
subject does not need to know the concrete types of its observers.

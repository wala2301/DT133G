Agile software development is an iterative approach to building software that emphasises delivering working software in short cycles, collaborating with customers, and responding to change.
The Agile Manifesto (2001) defines four values: individuals and interactions over processes and tools; working software over comprehensive documentation; customer collaboration over contract negotiation; and responding to change over following a plan. Twelve principles follow from these values, including delivering working software frequently, welcoming changing requirements, and maintaining a sustainable pace.
Agile methods include Scrum, Kanban, Extreme Programming (XP), and SAFe. They share a focus on iterative delivery, frequent feedback, and cross-functional teams. Retrospectives at the end of each iteration allow teams to inspect their process and improve continuously.
Agile does not mean skipping documentation or design; it means doing these things in proportion to the need and adapting them as requirements evolve rather than following a fixed plan set at the project's start.

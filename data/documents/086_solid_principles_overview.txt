SOLID is an acronym for five object-oriented design principles that aim to produce
software that is easier to maintain and extend. The five principles are: Single
Responsibility, Open/Closed, Liskov Substitution, Interface Segregation, and Dependency
Inversion. Applying SOLID principles tends to produce smaller, more cohesive classes and
loose coupling between modules. These principles are widely taught as software engineering
best practices and are especially relevant when designing systems intended to evolve over
time.

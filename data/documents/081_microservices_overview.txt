Microservices architecture structures an application as a collection of small,
independently deployable services, each responsible for a specific business capability.
Services communicate over well-defined APIs, typically HTTP REST or message queues, and
can be developed, deployed, and scaled independently. This contrasts with a monolithic
architecture where all functionality is bundled into a single deployable unit.
Microservices enable technology diversity and team autonomy but introduce operational
complexity around service discovery, distributed tracing, and data consistency across
service boundaries.

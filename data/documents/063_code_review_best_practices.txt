Code review is a systematic examination of source code by one or more developers other
than the author, aimed at finding defects and improving quality before merging. Effective
reviews focus on correctness, clarity, test coverage, and adherence to project
conventions rather than style preferences, which should be enforced automatically by
linters. Reviewers should provide specific, actionable, and constructive feedback and
distinguish between blocking issues and optional suggestions. Research by Rigby and Bird
found that smaller, more frequent reviews under 400 lines of changed code are more
effective at finding defects than large, infrequent ones.

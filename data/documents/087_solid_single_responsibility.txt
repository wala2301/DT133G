The Single Responsibility Principle (SRP) states that a class should have only one reason
to change, meaning it should be responsible for exactly one part of the software's
functionality. When a class handles multiple concerns, a change in one area risks breaking
unrelated functionality and makes the class harder to understand and test. In practice,
SRP encourages splitting large classes into smaller focused ones. For example, separating
data access logic from business logic and presentation logic is a direct application of
SRP and is reflected in layered architectures.

A well-written Git commit message communicates the intent of a change and serves as a
permanent record in the project history. The widely adopted convention is to write a
short subject line of 50 characters or fewer in the imperative mood, such as "Fix null
pointer in auth handler", optionally followed by a blank line and a more detailed body.
The body should explain what changed and why, not how, since the diff shows the how.
Conventional Commits is a specification that extends this with type prefixes such as
feat, fix, docs, or chore to enable automated changelog generation and semantic version
bumping.

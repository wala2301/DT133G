HTTP status codes are three-digit numbers returned by a server to indicate the result
of a request. Codes in the 2xx range indicate success, with 200 OK being the most
common. The 3xx range is used for redirections, 4xx for client errors such as 404
Not Found and 401 Unauthorized, and 5xx for server-side errors. Status codes help
clients understand whether a request succeeded and what follow-up action to take.
